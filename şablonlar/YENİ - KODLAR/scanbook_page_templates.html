ABOUT THIS SCAN

What is a private MRI scan?

An MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) scan uses powerful magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the inside of your body — without using ionising radiation. It is one of the most comprehensive and versatile diagnostic tools in modern medicine.

Unlike CT scans or X-rays, MRI is particularly effective at imaging soft tissues, including the brain, spinal cord, muscles, ligaments, and internal organs. It is the preferred method for diagnosing conditions of the spine, joints, and neurological system.

With ScanBook, you can access 1.5T standard MRI or 3T high-field MRI — recommended for complex cases such as prostate imaging, neurological conditions, or when superior image resolution is required.

FAQ

Common questions about private MRI

Do I need a GP referral for a private MRI?
+
No. All ScanBook partner centres accept self-referrals. You can book directly without a letter from your GP. However, if you already have a referral, you're welcome to bring it — your radiologist may find it helpful.
How much does a private MRI scan cost?
+
Private MRI scans on ScanBook start from £199. Prices vary depending on the body part, scanner type (1.5T vs 3T), and location. All prices shown include the scan and a full written radiologist report.
How quickly will I get my results?
+
Most partner centres deliver a full written radiologist report within 24–48 hours of your scan. Some centres offer same-day or next-day reports. The report will be emailed directly to you as a PDF.
What is the difference between 1.5T and 3T MRI?
+
1.5T is the standard field strength and is suitable for the majority of diagnostic scans. 3T (Tesla) provides higher resolution images and is recommended for complex cases — particularly prostate cancer screening (mpMRI), neurological conditions, and sports injury assessment. ScanBook filters centres by scanner type so you can choose the right option for your needs.
Is it safe to have a private MRI scan?
+
Yes. MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves — not ionising radiation — making it one of the safest imaging technologies available. It is not suitable for patients with certain metallic implants, pacemakers, or cochlear implants. You will be asked to complete a safety questionnaire before your scan.
