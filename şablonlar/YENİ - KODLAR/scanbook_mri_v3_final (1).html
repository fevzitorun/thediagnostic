ABOUT THIS SCAN

What does an MRI scan show?

An MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) scan uses powerful magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the inside of the body. It produces no ionising radiation and is particularly effective at imaging soft tissues, including the brain, spinal cord, muscles, ligaments and internal organs.

MRI is the preferred method for diagnosing conditions of the spine, joints and brain. It is also used for specialist scans including multiparametric MRI for prostate assessment, cardiac MRI, and breast imaging.

ON THE DAY

What to expect
at your appointment

All ScanBook partner centres are CQC registered. You will be greeted by an experienced radiographer who will walk you through the process before the scan begins.

1
Arriving at the centre
Arrive 10 minutes before your appointment. You will complete a short safety questionnaire and remove any metal items before entering the scan room.
2
The scan
You will lie on a motorised table that moves into the scanner. Most scans take between 20 and 45 minutes. Ear protection is always provided.
3
Contrast injections
Some scans use a contrast agent to improve image clarity. Your centre will advise you in advance if this is required for your scan.
4
Your radiologist's report
A consultant radiologist will examine your images and produce a written report, emailed to you within 24 to 48 hours. You may share this directly with your GP.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common questions about
private MRI

Do I need a GP referral to book a private MRI scan?
+
No. All ScanBook partner centres accept self-referrals. You can book directly online without a letter from your GP. If you already have a referral letter, you are welcome to bring it as your radiologist may find it helpful.
How much does a private MRI scan cost?
+
Private MRI scans on ScanBook start from £275 for one body part. Prices are fixed: 1 body part £275, 2 body parts £445, 3 body parts £575, 4 body parts £725, and Full Body MRI £1,450. All prices include the scan and a full written radiologist's report with no hidden fees.
How soon will I receive my results?
+
Most partner centres deliver a full written radiologist's report within 24 to 48 hours of your scan. The report will be emailed to you directly as a PDF. Some centres offer same-day reports for urgent cases.
Is a private MRI scan safe?
+
Yes. MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves rather than ionising radiation, making it one of the safest forms of diagnostic imaging. It is not suitable for patients with certain metallic implants, pacemakers or cochlear implants. You will be asked to complete a safety questionnaire before your scan takes place.
Can I share my MRI report with my NHS GP?
+
Yes. Your report is delivered as a standard PDF and your digital images can be downloaded in DICOM format. Both can be shared directly with your NHS GP or any specialist you are seeing. Many patients find that having their own imaging speeds up their NHS care considerably.
Can I scan more than one body part in a single appointment?
+
Yes. ScanBook offers fixed pricing for multiple body parts scanned in a single session. You can book 2, 3 or 4 body parts at a discounted combined price, or choose a Full Body MRI for comprehensive whole-body screening.
