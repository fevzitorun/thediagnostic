ABOUT THIS SCAN

What does an MRI scan show?

An MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) scan uses powerful magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the inside of the body. Unlike CT scans or X-rays, it produces no ionising radiation and is particularly effective at imaging soft tissues.

MRI is the preferred method for diagnosing conditions of the spine, joints, brain, and abdomen. It is also used for specialist scans such as multiparametric MRI for prostate cancer assessment, cardiac MRI, and breast imaging.

At ScanBook, you can choose between standard 1.5 Tesla and high-field 3 Tesla scanners. 3T is recommended for complex cases where finer resolution makes a clinical difference.

ON THE DAY

What to expect
at your appointment

1
Arriving at the centre
Arrive 10 minutes before your appointment. You will be asked to complete a short safety questionnaire and remove any metal items.
2
The scan itself
You will lie on a motorised table that moves into the scanner. Most scans take between 20 and 45 minutes. The machine produces loud knocking sounds; ear protection is provided.
3
Contrast injections
Some scans use a contrast agent administered intravenously to improve image clarity. Your centre will advise you in advance if this applies to your scan.
4
Your radiologist's report
A consultant radiologist will examine your images and produce a written report, which is emailed to you within 24 to 48 hours. You are welcome to share this directly with your GP.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common questions about
private MRI

Do I need a GP referral to book a private MRI scan?
+
No. All ScanBook partner centres accept self-referrals. You can book directly online without a letter from your GP. If you already have a referral letter, you are welcome to bring it as your radiologist may find it helpful.
How much does a private MRI scan cost?
+
Private MRI scans on ScanBook start from £199. Prices vary depending on the body part, scanner type (1.5T or 3T) and location. All prices shown include the scan and a full written radiologist's report. There are no hidden fees.
How soon will I receive my results?
+
Most partner centres deliver a full written radiologist's report within 24 to 48 hours of your scan. The report will be emailed to you directly as a PDF. Some centres offer same-day reports for urgent cases.
What is the difference between a 1.5T and a 3T MRI scanner?
+
1.5 Tesla is the standard field strength and is suitable for the majority of diagnostic scans. 3 Tesla provides higher resolution images and is recommended for specific clinical situations, particularly prostate cancer screening (multiparametric MRI), neurological conditions and complex sports injuries. ScanBook allows you to filter centres by scanner type.
Is a private MRI scan safe?
+
Yes. MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves rather than ionising radiation, making it one of the safest forms of diagnostic imaging. It is not suitable for patients with certain metallic implants, pacemakers or cochlear implants. You will be asked to complete a safety questionnaire before your scan takes place.
Can I share my MRI report with my NHS GP?
+
Yes. Your report is delivered as a standard PDF and your digital images can be downloaded in DICOM format. Both can be shared directly with your NHS GP or any specialist you are seeing. Many patients find that having their own imaging speeds up their NHS care considerably.
